Like Cat and Dog) announces its intentions through title alone. This isn't reverential Shakespeare performed in period costume with hushed tones and tragic inevitability. It's a reframing that acknowledges what anyone who has actually read "Romeo and Juliet" knows: these aren't star-crossed lovers doomed by fatethey're impulsive teenagers whose families' dysfunction turns adolescent passion into deadly consequences.

The title's metaphor"like cat and dog"works on multiple levels. On surface, it suggests constant fighting, the inability to coexist peacefully, relationships defined by conflict rather than harmony. But anyone who has watched actual cats and dogs knows their relationships are more complex: sometimes antagonistic, sometimes playful, often both simultaneously. This ambiguity better captures Shakespeare's text than romantic mythology that has accumulated around it through centuries of misreading and selective adaptation.

Greece's relationship with Shakespeare carries particular complexity. Classical Greek theater invented dramatic forms that Shakespeare later adapted, creating circular influence where modern Greek productions of Shakespeare reconnect with theatrical traditions that predate him. When Greek artists approach Shakespeare, they bring perspectives shaped by tragic traditions older than anything England produced, by cultural understanding of family honor and vendetta that makes Capulet-Montague feuding feel less exotic than American or Northern European directors might assume.

The November-through-March timing positions this production across Athens' theatrical season, when audiences seek substance over spectacle, when extended contemplation of human dysfunction feels appropriate to darker months. "Romeo and Juliet" as romantic tragedy offers escapist fantasy. "Romeo and Juliet" as study of how families destroy children through pride, how communities enable violence through failure to intervene, how passion without wisdom leads to preventable deaththis version demands engagement with uncomfortable truths that resonate regardless of historical period or cultural context.
