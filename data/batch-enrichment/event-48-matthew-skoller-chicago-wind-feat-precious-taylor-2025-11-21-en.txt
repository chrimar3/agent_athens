Matthew Skoller & Chicago Wind featuring Precious Taylor bring authentic Chicago blues to Athens for an exceptional four-night residency at Half Note Jazz Club from November 21-24, 2025. This rare extended engagement showcases one of Chicago's most respected harmonica players and bandleaders alongside powerhouse vocalist Precious Taylor, delivering the raw energy and sophisticated musicianship that defines the Chicago blues tradition.

Matthew Skoller stands as a torchbearer of Chicago's legendary blues harmonica lineage, having learned directly from masters who shaped the genre's golden age. His deep, expressive tone and commanding stage presence have earned him recognition across the international blues circuit. Leading Chicago Wind, his carefully assembled ensemble of virtuoso musicians, Skoller creates a sound that honors tradition while pushing the music forward with contemporary vitality. Half Note Jazz Club provides the ideal setting for this blues summit. Since opening its doors in 1992, this legendary 200-capacity venue in the Mets neighborhood has established itself as Athens' premier destination for authentic jazz and blues performances. The intimate space creates an electric connection between artists and audience, where every harmonica wail, guitar bend, and vocal inflection resonates with clarity and power. The club's exceptional acoustics and sophisticated atmosphere have attracted world-class musicians for over three decades, building a reputation that extends far beyond Greece's borders.

The Mets neighborhood, situated southeast of central Athens near the iconic Panathenaic Stadium, offers the perfect complement to an evening of Chicago blues. This residential area combines classical architecture with contemporary cultural venues, creating an authentic Athenian atmosphere away from tourist crowds. The tree-lined streets and local tavernas provide opportunities to extend your evening with post-concert conversations and late-night dining.

This four-night engagement follows an unusual schedule that accommodates both weekend and weekday audiences. Friday and Saturday performances begin at 22:30, allowing for leisurely dinners before the music starts. Sunday and Monday shows start earlier at 21:30, making weeknight attendance more practical while maintaining the venue's intimate late-night ambiance.

Whether you're a devoted blues enthusiast or simply curious about Chicago's musical heritage, these concerts promise authentic performances that capture the genre's emotional power and technical sophistication.
