On November 6th at 20:00, TV Control Center in Kypseli hosts an evening of experimental sound featuring two distinctive acts from opposite sides of the Atlantic. The Budokan Boys, a duo from the USA, arrive in Athens to present their new album "THE OOZE," with French percussionist Eiger Drums Propaganda opening the night with a solo drum set performance.

TV Control Center, known locally as KET, occupies a former television repair shop from the 1970s at the intersection of Kyprou 91A and Sikinou 35A. Since 2012, this intimate 80-capacity space has served as a gathering point for Athens' experimental arts community, hosting performing arts, documentary screenings, and music that pushes beyond conventional boundaries. The venue sits near Agios Nikolaos metro station, making it accessible while retaining the residential character that defines Kypseli's creative underground.

The Budokan Boys bring "THE OOZE" to this compact setting, where the proximity between performers and audience creates a different listening experience than larger venues allow. Working as a duo, they've crafted an album that benefits from the kind of focused attention a room of 80 people can provide. The intimacy of KET means every textural detail, every dynamic shift registers with clarity.

Opening the evening, Eiger Drums Propaganda presents a solo drum set performance. Working alone with percussion, the French artist explores rhythm as both structure and material, treating the drum kit as a complete instrument rather than a backing element.
